Core project facts:
- Company: FinFlow, a neobank.
- User base in business case: 2 million users.
- Business issue: around 8% customer churn every quarter.
- Dataset size: 1000 synthetic customer records.
- Dataset churn distribution: 820 non-churned customers, 180 churned customers.
- Dataset churn rate: 18%.
- Target column: Churned, where 0 = retained and 1 = churned.
- Model restriction: no pre-trained models, no external ML APIs, no AutoML, no downloaded models.
- Models trained from scratch: Decision Tree Classifier and Gradient Boosting Classifier.

- Best model metrics: Accuracy 0.8950, Precision 0.7143, Recall 0.6944, F1-score 0.7042, ROC-AUC 0.9443.
- Decision Tree metrics: Accuracy 0.7850, Precision 0.4493, Recall 0.8611, F1-score 0.5905, ROC-AUC 0.8962.
- Top churn drivers from Gradient Boosting: Last_Login_Days_Ago, Avg_Monthly_Transactions, Support_Tickets.
- High-risk customers identified: 160.
- Medium-risk customers identified: 30.
- Low-risk customers identified: 810.
- High-priority customers identified: 32.
- Medium-priority customers identified: 158.
- Low-priority customers identified: 810.
- Top-20 save-list CLV: INR 79,410.05.

Slide 6: Modeling Approach
Title:
Two Explainable Models Trained From Scratch

Bullets:
- Decision Tree: interpretable baseline model.
- Gradient Boosting: stronger model for non-linear churn patterns.
- Train-test split: 80% train, 20% test.
- Stratified sampling preserved churn class distribution.
- Missing numeric values handled with median imputation.

Why these models:
- Suitable for tabular customer behavior data.
- Easy to explain using feature importance.
- No pre-trained models or external APIs used.

Slide 9: CLV and Retention Prioritization
Title:
From Prediction to Action

CLV formula:
CLV = Avg_Monthly_Revenue x Expected_Months

Expected months formula:
Expected_Months = max(1, 12 - Last_Login_Days_Ago / 30)

Priority formula:
Priority_Score = Churn_Probability x CLV

Customer segments:
- High Priority: high churn probability and top-value CLV.
- Medium Priority: moderate or high churn probability, but not top-value CLV.
- Low Priority: lower churn risk.

Output results:
- High Priority: 32 customers.
- Medium Priority: 158 customers.
- Low Priority: 810 customers.
