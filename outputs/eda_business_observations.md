# EDA Business Observations

- Customers who churned had a higher average number of days since last login, which suggests inactivity is an important warning signal.
- Customers who churned had a lower feature usage score, which suggests weaker product engagement.
- Customers who churned had more support tickets on average, which may indicate service friction or unresolved dissatisfaction.
- Customers who churned had lower monthly transaction activity on average, which suggests reduced banking habit formation.
- Revenue helps separate churn risk from business value, so it is important for retention prioritization even when it is not the strongest churn predictor.
- The largest mean difference between churned and retained customers is in Last_Login_Days_Ago.
